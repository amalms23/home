About

Welcome to my homepage! My name is Amal M S, and I'm currently pursuing my Ph.D. in Aerospace Engineering with a focus on Computational Mechanics at IIT Kanpur, Uttar Pradesh. I'm deeply passionate about pushing the boundaries of Computational Materials Design & Analysis at the Artificially Engineered Materials and Structures (AEMS) Lab within the Department of Aerospace Engineering. My research interests primarily lie in the field of Mechanical Metamaterials, where I explore the computational study of active and programmable property modulation. By architecting micro-level geometries, I aim to enhance material properties and achieve multifunctionality, ultimately developing materials with properties that defy conventional engineering limitations. Originally from Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala, India, I completed my Bachelor's degree in Production Engineering from NIT Tiruchirappalli, Tamil Nadu. Throughout my academic journey, I have developed a strong foundation in Computer Aided Design (CAD), Finite Element Methods (FEM), Numerical Methods, and Structural Analysis. I believe that these skills, coupled with my research focus, position me well to make significant contributions to the field of Aerospace Engineering and Computational Mechanics. Beyond my academic pursuits, I am fascinated by the intersection of art and engineering ingenuity. I actively explore the potential of origami and kirigami to create novel materials and structures, adding a creative dimension to the world of metamaterials. With a commitment to innovation and a drive to explore new frontiers, I am excited about the possibilities that lie ahead in my academic and professional journey.
